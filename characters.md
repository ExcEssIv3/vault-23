# Vault 23 — Characters

See [lore.md](lore.md) for the premise these characters are reacting to.

**The Overseer (father) — George IX**: family patriarch, adamant against outside contact/diversity — sees the radiation program as the vault's own internal "family" solution, outsiders as contamination of the bloodline. Deliberately *not* written as denial or self-delusion — the program has real successes, some children genuinely are born healthier with more diversity, and it demonstrably works some of the time. Central moral question: sometimes it works — does that justify the deaths and suffering incurred getting there? His resistance to outsiders is also legitimate, not paranoid — the wasteland is genuinely dangerous, and it's a real, justified risk, not just purity rhetoric. He's balancing a known, contained cost he can control against an unknown, potentially existential one.

**The son (usurper/heir apparent) — George X**: opposes his father, believes outside genetic diversity is the fix. His view isn't a prior proposal that got overruled — it's simpler: the genetic decline is obvious, and his father isn't doing anything real about it, as far as he knows. He doesn't know about the radiation program, and doesn't know he himself is a product of it (born healthy, early in the program) — that ignorance is the dramatic irony at the center of his character, resolved later in a confrontation where he learns the truth. Currently an isolated dissenter, likely without organized allies, frustrated by inaction he can see but can't explain.

Naming convention: eldest son of the eldest son has always been named George, matching the fixed succession rule (see [lore.md](lore.md)) — royalty-style regnal numbering rather than personal names. ~200 years and 9 generations puts the current Overseer at George IX and the heir at George X, roughly a 22-year average generation gap, in line with historical dynastic succession.

**The Overseer's wife (mother) — Rebecca**: knows the truth, part of the small circle. Stopped having children after her healthy son — a knowing choice, not coincidence. Currently aligned with the father's position, but persuadable — the clearest swing vote in the story. Can be convinced to side with the son, or to broker a halt to the program without full regime change. Keeps her own given name rather than being folded into the numbered-George convention — a small but pointed contrast to how the men's identities collapse into sameness.

**The doctor — Silas**: a true believer, Mengele-archetype. Not conflicted, not coerced — he genuinely enjoys the experimentation for its own sake. No guilt, no internal arc needed; his coldness is the disturbing part. Serves as a moral fixed point with zero ambiguity, in contrast to the Overseer. Name choice leans into an old-fashioned, puritanical Anglo-American register (matching George/Rebecca) rather than a German name — despite the Mengele archetype, he's part of the same single bloodline as everyone else in the vault, so the "one heritage" constraint rules out foreign-coded naming; the coldness comes through in characterization, not nomenclature.

**Clyde**: the "hero" the vault sent out before the player arrived. Sent to find reactor parts needed to reseal the leak, with no real idea what he's looking for or how to find it — rants to the player about the impossible, humiliating position he was put in. Not brave, not competent. Reached Vault 21, became infatuated with Sarah, and — out of fear of the wasteland and social cowardice — never left and never approached her. Found seated in Vault 21's dining room, a talkable NPC fixture requiring no quest marker.

**Teresa**: Silas's assistant. Suspicious of him but not forthcoming by default — a high enough Speech/Barter check gets her to reveal he keeps a locked safe in his office, or the player can earn the same information by completing a quest for her instead. Introduced in the beat right after the ghoul incident, when the player goes to confront Silas about the "autopsy" and gets stonewalled. See [story-beats.md §3.6](story-beats.md#36-silass-office--teresa).
