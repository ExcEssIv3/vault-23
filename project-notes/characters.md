# Vault 23 — Characters

See [lore.md](lore.md) for the premise these characters are reacting to.

**The Overseer (father) — George IX**: family patriarch, adamant against outside contact/diversity — sees the radiation program as the vault's own internal "family" solution, outsiders as contamination of the bloodline. Deliberately *not* written as denial or self-delusion — the program has real successes, some children genuinely are born healthier with more diversity, and it demonstrably works some of the time. Central moral question: sometimes it works — does that justify the deaths and suffering incurred getting there? His resistance to outsiders is also legitimate, not paranoid — the wasteland is genuinely dangerous, and it's a real, justified risk, not just purity rhetoric. He's balancing a known, contained cost he can control against an unknown, potentially existential one.

**The son (usurper/heir apparent) — George X**: opposes his father, believes outside genetic diversity is the fix. His view isn't a prior proposal that got overruled — it's simpler: the genetic decline is obvious, and his father isn't doing anything real about it, as far as he knows. He doesn't know about the radiation program, and doesn't know he himself is a product of it (born healthy, early in the program) — that ignorance is the dramatic irony at the center of his character, resolved later in a confrontation where he learns the truth. Currently an isolated dissenter, likely without organized allies, frustrated by inaction he can see but can't explain.

Naming convention: eldest son of the eldest son has always been named George, matching the fixed succession rule (see [lore.md](lore.md)) — royalty-style regnal numbering rather than personal names. ~200 years and 9 generations puts the current Overseer at George IX and the heir at George X, roughly a 22-year average generation gap, in line with historical dynastic succession.

**The Overseer's wife (mother) — Rebecca**: knows the truth, part of the small circle. Stopped having children after her healthy son — a knowing choice, not coincidence. Currently aligned with the father's position, but persuadable — the clearest swing vote in the story. Can be convinced to side with the son, or to broker a halt to the program without full regime change. Keeps her own given name rather than being folded into the numbered-George convention — a small but pointed contrast to how the men's identities collapse into sameness.

**The doctor — Silas**: a true believer, Mengele-archetype. Not conflicted, not coerced — he genuinely enjoys the experimentation for its own sake. No guilt, no internal arc needed; his coldness is the disturbing part. Serves as a moral fixed point with zero ambiguity, in contrast to the Overseer. Name choice leans into an old-fashioned, puritanical Anglo-American register (matching George/Rebecca) rather than a German name — despite the Mengele archetype, he's part of the same single bloodline as everyone else in the vault, so the "one heritage" constraint rules out foreign-coded naming; the coldness comes through in characterization, not nomenclature. Trust-building task (see [story-beats.md §3.2](story-beats.md#32-arrival--earning-trust-inside-vault-23)): a mundane RadAway/RadX resupply request, plus an optional, shadier side ask for irradiated ghoul brain matter "for testing" — a first, deniable hint of his real nature, well before the ghoul incident makes it land.

**Clyde**: the "hero" the vault sent out before the player arrived. Sent to find reactor parts needed to reseal the leak, with no real idea what he's looking for or how to find it — rants to the player about the impossible, humiliating position he was put in. Not brave, not competent. Reached Vault 21, became infatuated with Sarah, and — out of fear of the wasteland and social cowardice — never left and never approached her. Found seated in Vault 21's dining room, a talkable NPC fixture requiring no quest marker. Back in Vault 23, his day job is janitor, alongside Agnes — unmarried, which tracks with why he was the one expendable enough to send out in the first place. If the player never resolves his §3.1 thread, he simply never leaves Vault 21: sits in its dining room every day and sleeps there, permanently. If he's brought back (or his key taken by force), he returns to his janitorial duties in Vault 23.

**Phil**: the vault's armorer, a gun nut who's built an anti-materiel rifle off a schematic he found in the vault library, working from a bench that also holds a Mr. Handy he's been scrapping for parts. Trust-building task (see [story-beats.md §3.2](story-beats.md#32-arrival--earning-trust-inside-vault-23)): fetch scrap metal/electronics/a fission battery to finish the build, jury-riggable with a high Repair check, plus an optional side goal of teaching him to shoot it.

**Haley**: the vault's lead chef, runs the kitchen with a couple of other dwellers under her. Trust-building task (see [story-beats.md §3.2](story-beats.md#32-arrival--earning-trust-inside-vault-23)): the dwellers are sick of the same generations-old recipes and rations, so she asks the Courier for wasteland ingredients — fruit, meat, and spices — to vary the menu. Optionally rounded out by buying her a cookbook from the Crimson Caravan, or teaching her the recipes directly with a high enough Survival skill.

**Teresa**: Silas's assistant. Suspicious of him but not willing to act on suspicion alone — a high enough Speech check convinces her outright; failing it sends the player after physical proof instead (a tissue sample from the incinerated ghoul's body, cut from the vault freezer). Either way she ends up revealing the password to Silas's computer, which opens his office safe. Introduced in the beat right after the ghoul incident, when the player goes to confront Silas about the "autopsy" and gets stonewalled. See [story-beats.md §3.6](story-beats.md#36-silass-office--teresa).

**Player-facing NPCs for trust-building**: the doctor, whoever runs the armory, someone in the kitchen — see [story-beats.md](story-beats.md#32-arrival--earning-trust-inside-vault-23).
