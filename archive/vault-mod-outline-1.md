# Vault [TBD] — Concept Outline

## Core premise (the experiment)
- Vault-Tec's experiment: seal a **single family** in the vault, with the patriarch as Overseer.
- The family is expected to interbreed and repopulate the vault over generations.
- 200 years later, the vault opens due to a crisis — population is large, but riddled with genetic defects (founder effect / inbreeding depression).

## Inspirations / tone references
- Habsburgs — dynastic intermarriage for power preservation, physical degeneration as a consequence of a deliberate choice, not an accident. Maps directly onto the patriarch's self-justification for keeping the bloodline "pure."
- Mad Max War Boys — treated as a *separate, compounding* aesthetic layer (pallor, ritual, founder-worship from generations without real sunlight), not as the cause of the genetic issues. Two distinct vault horrors: isolation + inbreeding.

## The twist (revealed mid/late)
- The current generation recognized the genetic decline.
- Their "fix": crack open the vault's reactor and irradiate babies, hoping radiation induces beneficial mutation / diversity (Fallout-style pseudo-science, consistent with FEV/ghouls/mutants logic — not real-world biology).
- This requires a high birth rate to run enough trials → **all childbearing women in the vault are pregnant**. This is the first thing the player notices, before understanding why.
- Side effect: radiation leakage is worsening and now threatens the whole vault. They need outside expertise to reseal the reactor.

## Structure: FO1 homage
- Vault sends out a "hero" — a healthy adult male generalist — to find someone capable of resealing/fixing the reactor.
- He is not brave and not especially competent. He reaches **Vault 21**, becomes infatuated with Sarah, and — out of fear of the wasteland and social cowardice — never leaves and never approaches her.
- Courier finds him seated in Vault 21's dining room (no active quest marker needed to discover him — a talkable NPC fixture there).
- He explains the situation at a surface level only: his vault needs a scientist/mechanic/capable generalist. No mention of radiation or babies — that's discovered on-site.
- He gives the vault's location (no need to make finding it a challenge) — this simply marks the vault on the map.
- He does not accompany the Courier back (fear-driven refusal); optional epilogue beat where the Courier can needle/drag him into finally confronting the outcome.

## Vault power dynamics
- Entire population self-identifies as **one family**.
- **Current Overseer** = family patriarch. Adamant against outside contact/diversity — sees the radiation program as the vault's own internal, "family" solution; outsiders = contamination of the bloodline.
- **Heir apparent** (likely his son) opposes him — believes outside genetic diversity is the actual fix. Proposed this *before* the father moved to the radiation program; was overruled.
- Succession mechanic (working direction): father designates his successor, but the son is the *expected* heir — raising the stakes, since the father could disinherit his own presumptive heir over this ideological rift rather than it being a clean, low-risk handoff.
- Son's current position: isolated dissenter, likely without organized allies, possibly suspects deformities/deaths are worse than officially acknowledged but lacks proof.
- Player's role: can investigate and bring the son proof, turning him from a passive dissenter into someone willing to actually move against his father — making the Courier the actual instigator of the coup, not just a bystander picking a side.
- One resolution path: overthrow/depose the current Overseer, install the son, open the vault to outside genetic diversity as the long-term fix.

## Open questions / next to flesh out
1. Father's psychology: does he believe the radiation program is working (points to a "successful" case), or is he in denial/founder-worship, doubling down rather than admit failure?
2. Is there a mother/matriarch figure — dead, complicit, secretly allied with the son, or a third position entirely (e.g., seal the vault forever, accept extinction rather than either option)?
3. Exact succession mechanism/ritual, if any (pure designation vs. some vestigial "fitness" trial — ironic given the vault's genetic state).
4. Son's support base, if any, inside the vault.
5. Evidence trail the player uncovers on-site (deformities, terminal logs, stillbirths, a "successful" mutant child) before meeting either the Overseer or the son.
6. Full win/lose branch structure (to be tackled after the above is locked).
